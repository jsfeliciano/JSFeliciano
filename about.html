Education & Work

Graduating high school in 2005, I immediately enrolled in a two year degree program at the Katherine Gibbs College of Art located in Norwalk, CT. By summer of 2008, I graduated and received an Associates Degree in Visual Communications.

Following graduation, 2008-2013, I began working alongside local print and event marketing groups as a graphic designer. During this time I gained a multitude of experience working in both digital and print. By 2010, freelancing opportunities would begin to present themselves regularly.

MY STORY

As I child, I was always fascinated by books, drawing, and stories of all kinds. Reading easily was my favorite past time. Noticing this, my mother kept a constant supply of books around as I would easily chew through 200+ pages in a weekend.

During my early teens, the internet was begining to roll out in it's infant state (AOL, Netscape Navigator). I would spend these years literally growing up alongside the internet exploring everything it had to offer. Over time, I found myself regulary interacting in creative internet forums and communities.

By 2005, I had taken a large interest in Adobe Photoshop, just as the internet began to explode. At this point designing became a part of daily life.
